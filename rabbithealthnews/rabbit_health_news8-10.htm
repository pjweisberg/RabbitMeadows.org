Breaking News from Washington State

Raccoon & Skunk roundworm parasite

  There appears to be new hope on the horizon for rabbits who likely have raccoon roundworm (cerebral larava migrams). Long-term treatment with oxibendazole (4 times higher dose than is being used to treat E. cuniculi) has shown some success holding at bay the devastation to the brain caused by this parasite. Two rabbits with a likely diagnosis (after E.cuniculi, middle ear infection, spinal injury, etc. were ruled out) of raccoon roundworm have been receiving this experimental treatment for ten months. In previously diagnosed cases, rabbits with this parasite have lived only three to four months after symptoms first appeared. Diagnosis was confirmed on autopsy. Treatment seems to have already significantly extended life, with quality, for these two lucky rabbits.
