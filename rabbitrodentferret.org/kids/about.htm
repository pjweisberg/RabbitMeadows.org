STATEMENT OF PURPOSE
The Youth House Rabbit Club is an all-volunteer organization with
three primary goals: 1) To educate young people on rabbits as companion animals 2) To assist the HRS and rescued rabbits by doing volunteer work and fundraising 3)To provide a place where young rabbit lovers can correspond and meet with others who have house rabbits.

PHILOSOPHY & GENERAL POLICIES
1. TheYouth House Rabbit Club believes that ALL rabbits are valuable as
individuals, regardless of breed purity, temperament, state of health, or
relationship to humans. Educating young people on the welfare of all rabbits is our primary consideration.

2. We follow the House Rabbit Society's philosophy: domestic rabbits are dependent on humans and are companion animals; it is in the best interest of domestic rabbits to be neutered/spayed, and live in human housing in which supervision, protection, attention, mental stimulation, exercise, and social interaction are provided.

See HRS' philosophy and policies http://www.rabbit.org/hrs-info/philosophy.html and http://www.rabbit.org/hrs-info/policies.html

3. The Youth House Rabbit Club does not support or align itself with any group or individual promoting rabbits as meat, fur, or show animals.


WHAT THIS CLUB IS ABOUT

1. Learning about rabbits as companion animals

2. Volunteering for the HRS

3. Meeting and corresponding with other young rabbit lovers

4. Increasing public awareness of house rabbits and HRS

5. Having fun!


WHAT THIS CLUB IS NOT ABOUT

1. Owning rabbits for financial profit

2. Using rabbits as food animals

3. Breeding or showing rabbits
