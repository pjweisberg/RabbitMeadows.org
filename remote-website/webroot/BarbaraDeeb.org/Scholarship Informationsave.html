Barbara Deeb Veterinary Scholarship Program

Established by Best Little Rabbit, Rodent & Rodent House (BLRRFH) & House Rabbit Society

For DVM candidates with a sincere interest in the special needs of rabbits, guinea pigs, rodents & ferrets (Small Exotics)

“Fundamentally, there are four guiding principles for veterinarians:

(1) To pursue the work of their profession with uprightness of conduct. (2) To serve the community to the utmost of their ability. (3) To constantly endeavor to ensure the welfare of animals committed to their care. (4) To seek no personal advantage at the expense of their professional colleagues.”

Purpose

The purpose of the Barbara Deeb Scholarship Program is to encourage veterinary students to pursue the study of the special needs and problems of Small Exotics. Barbara Deeb Veterinary Scholarship Program can provide scholarships to qualified individuals interested in this specialization. Applicants will benefit from volunteering with qualified 501(c)3 non-profit organizations specializing in the rescue and placement of Small Exotics while they complete their education and will be better prepared to help Small Exotics later encountered in their day-to-day practice

Application Guidelines

Selection of students to qualify for the scholarships is by individual application and is premised on their ongoing participation in a Small Exotics organization. Selection will be based upon the applicant’s demonstrated academic ability as well as economic need. The recipients will he selected by the Scholarship Committee. A recipient cannot he related to any “disqualified persons” of the Committee. Barbara Deeb Scholarship may award up to one scholarship per year to an individual pursuing a veterinary medicine degree with the purpose of improving the welfare of Small Exotics, provided they meet the following requirements:

• Applicant must have completed at least 18 months of continuous service (full or part-time) as a caregiver (employee or volunteer) of a nonprofit 501(c)3 as stated above.

• Applicant must have previously completed a minimum of a 4-year bachelors degree at an accredited college or university.

• Applicant must submit a Scholarship Plan detailing chronologically the academic requirements they must complete to achieve a doctorate in veterinary medicine. Costs must be detailed and totaled by year of study. If the Scholarship application is approved, the Scholarship Plan will serve as the disbursement guideline.

• Applicant must submit a personal statement outlining the reasons they are applying for the scholarship and how their education will benefit the Small Exotics community.

• Applications must be submitted no later than ????? for consideration for the following fail semester.

Funding Guidelines

• Scholarships must be renewed after the completion of each semester for continued funding. If all the Renewal Guidelines are not met, funding may be discontinued.

• Funds will he released as needed to pay for tuition, academic fees and textbooks. Funding is limited exclusively to academic costs related to attaining a veterinary medicine doctorate and does not extend to any elective academic pursuits.

• Payments must have detailed receipts supporting them and will be made directly to the educational or support organization on behalf of the recipient.

• Refunds for any reason of money originally paid out by the fund including dropping of a class are to be returned directly to the fund.

• The fund reserves the right to discontinue funding at any time if, in its sole opinion, the Recipient is not conforming to the Scholarship Plan or is not aggressively pursuing a specialization in Small Exotics.

Renewal Guidelines

Recipient will periodically provide original documentation and/or copies of the following items indicating that the recipient has completed the previous semester’s requirements and is on track with the Scholarship Plan submitted during the application process.

The following documents are due within 15 days of the end of each academic semester:

• Evidence of full-time enrollment for the upcoming term in an accredited veterinary medical program at an accredited university or college pre-approved by the committee or evidence of enrollment for required courses to enter veterinary school at a university or college pre-approved by the committee.

• Evidence of maintaining a satisfactory Grade Point Average by submission of school transcripts for the semester just ended.

Submission of an original research paper, prepared by the Recipient, on a topic of Small Exotic health or behavior (topic to be pre-approved by the committee), with the understanding that the research paper may be published by the committee.

The following documents are due within 30 days of the end of each calendar year:

• Proof of work or volunteer service with a 501(c)3 Small Exotic care organization, minimum of 175 hours during the calendar year just ended to verify that the Recipient continues to meet the volunteer/work requirement.
