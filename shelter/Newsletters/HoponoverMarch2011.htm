We Have Our Building Permit!

Here's an idea of what our new shelter will look like!

The building will be 38' x 48' with a second level loft of 14' x 48'. The first level will contain our reception area; adoption area with a place to spend time with potential companions; an education room; a boarding room; utility room; bathroom. The loft area will contain areas to quarantine incomming animals and those waiting to be spayed/neutered and a clinic room where animals can receive veterinary care.

Fundraising for the building: Create a a permanent memory by placing a picture of your favorite furry companion on our walls! Ceramic tiles will decorate our shelter walls with photos of your companion animal or you with your companion animal. Look for details on our web page within the next couple of weeks.

Volunteer: We are in immediate need of volunteers to help clear & prepare the building area. Some trees must come down, brush must be removed. New young fir trees need to be transplanted. We need advice about laying electric lines from the transformer to a temporary pole for the construction crew. Please contact us if you have a few hours available to help.
 

Boarding Services at Rabbit Meadows

We offer boarding services at our Redmond location at the most reasonable fees in the area: 
  • Rabbits: $10/day per cage  (out for daily exercise)
  • Guinea Pigs: $5/day per cage
  • Rodents (rats, mice, hamsters, gerbils): $2/day in your cage
Please do not make reservations "just in case" -- we would hate to turn others away unnecessarily and leave vacancies that reduce a precious source of funding for our rescue efforts. 
Drop-off times are between noon and 5pm Saturdays and Sundays.  Weekday drop-off is at the same time, but we need advance notice.

Always Include a Towel in Your Carrier

For your companion's safety and comfort, it is very important to place a folded towel inside your carrier when you and your companion travel anywhere.  Lately we have been seeing a lot of carriers arriving without towels, which means the rabbit, guinea pig, or rodent is not only having to undergo the normal stress of being carried around in a container, but also is sliding around inside with nothing soft to protect them.  Remember these little animals are sensitive and fragile; never leave home without a towel in your carrier!

Adorable, Adoptable Critters: here are just a couple of the wonderful animals awaiting their chance at a forever home

Meet Pepper, who is looking for his forever family...

Found as a stray in Bellingham, he was likely last year's "Easter Bunny" who got discarded when the "kids lost interest" or "he started spraying/marking everything!"  Now neutered, Pepper is the life of his party and is ready for lots of attention. 

Come visit Pepper at our Redmond Shelter. He's looking for someone who will spoil him with attention and love -- including letting him out in the house for several hours of exercise each day.

It's Fabio, the guinea-pig supermodel!

Fabio, here sporting a gorgeous new haircut, just turned one year old.  Time for a forever home!

Guinea Pigs with long hair need haircuts about every two months and need to be combed a minimum of once each week. If combed weekly (or daily as you get him out to play), no one will have to deal with matted fur (phew!). 

Fabio is sweet and vocal.  He enjoys lots of attention.  He would also love to find a girlfriend he can chat with when you're not around.
